James Baldwin

James Baldwin was an African American Author who was born on August 2nd, 1924. Since he grew up in the early 1900s he faced a lot of racism. This is demonstrated in his writing. In fact, one of the most common themes in all of his writing is racism.

Racism

Baldwin shows racism several times throughout his writing. A great example of this can be found in the essay "Notes of a native son". In this essay he describes how being an African American is being exactly like an unloved stepson. Although one(a black person) is no different then their siblings(a white person, the stepson is still looked upon as inferior.

Ta-nehisi Coates

Ta-nehisi Coates is an African American Author who was born on September 30, 1975. Similar to baldwin, Coates faced problems throughout his childhood. Coates' father had 7 children with 4 different women. Due to this, Coates grew up in povert. This can be shown through a major theme in his writing. Coates, often writes about poverty .

Poverty

Coates demonstrates the idea of poverty several times throughout his writing. A great example of this can be shown in the book "Between the world and me". In this book, Coates tells Samori that struggle is what allows one to live peacefully in this world. He says emphasises that the world is not an easy place and that it is better to just take struggle as something beneficial.

Common Themes

Both Baldwin and Coates experienced similar things in life. In fact, Coates was inspired by Baldwin to make the book "Between the world and Me". One of the most common themes throughout both of the author's writing is the general theme of struggle. For Baldwin, it was facing racism and for Coates it was living and growing up in poverty.
